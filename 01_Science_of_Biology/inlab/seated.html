Required: Full lab attendance mandatory. Be seated by 2:00 PM (Tues/Thur) or 9:00 AM (Wed).

Required Materials

  • Lab Manual: In 3-ring binder
  • Textbook: Campbell Biology (12th Ed.)
  • Electronic Computing Device: For recording lab work

Weekly Laboratory Flow

  1. Arrival: Store backpack/coat
  2. Hygiene: Wash hands (20-30 sec)
  3. Preparation: Organize desk
  4. Assessment: Weekly Quiz
  5. Work: Begin exercises
  6. Equipment: Check out microscope

Stop all activities at 4:20 PM (Tues/Thur) or 11:20 AM (Wed) for Metric conversions.

Policies

Missed Labs

  • Make-up Policy: Generally not allowed
  • Materials: Removed Friday morning each week
  • Reschedule: (See pg 4)

Pre-Lab Work

  • Answer Lab Manual questions before arrival
  • Attempt week's worksheet prior to session

Pre-Lab

Metacognitive Preparation

  • Tip: Write the lab title and provide a one-sentence synopsis based on what you infer from the title.
  • Identify what concepts you already understand and where uncertainty remains
  • Explain (in your own words) the purpose of the week’s lab
  • Predict which concept may challenge you during the lab
  • Note specific questions or gaps to address with the instructor during lab
  • Before lab, write a bulleted “action plan” of the steps you expect to perform; during lab, monitor progress by checking them off.

Grading & Exams

Written Quizzes

  • Total: 8 quizzes
  • Format: In Lab | 1 Attempt | 5 Minutes
  • Content: 10 questions (10 points)
    • 9 from previous lab
    • 1 from current week
    • Includes 2 practical questions

Exams

  • 1st Exam: Labs 1-5
  • 2nd Exam: Labs 6-11
  • Details: 125 Points | ~90 Questions (40 practical) | ~1 hour

Week 1 Agenda

  • Introduction: Course overview and expectations
  • Metric System: Conversions
  • Measurements:
    • Estimate prokaryotic/eukaryotic cell sizes
    • Determine field of view diameter
    • Measure your desk
  • Microscopy:
    • Measure the size of the diameter of the field of view of the 4x objective lens
    • Calculate the size of the diameter of the field of view of both the 10x & 40x objective lenses
    • View, count, and estimate the size of Anabaena cells
    • Prepare, view, count, and estimate the size of cheek cells
    • Compare the size of Anabaena cells to cheek cells
    • View and estimate the size of a Hydra organism
