Instructions

  1. View: the metric conversion tables 1st to understand the two tables.
  2. Learn: the words 'prefix' and 'base' as they relate to the metric system.
  3. Read: each question carefully paying attention to the prefix and base.
  4. Write: the question on your homework paper.
  5. Write: the conversion Type under the question. (Kilo is the only prefix above base in BIOS101 conversions)
  6. Step 1: Draw your "Train Tracks":
    • Write the starting number and unit (what you are converting from) in the first numerator (top) box.
    • In the second numerator (top) box, write the number 1 and the unit you are converting to.
    • In the denominator (2nd bottom box), write the number 10 and the unit you are converting from (this sets you up to add the exponent next).
  7. ⚠ This is the most frustrating step in metric conversions. Proceed with great patience and care.
  8. Step 1a: Which table should you use? Follow this decision tree:
    • Look at the TOP RIGHT box of your train track (the unit you're converting TO with the number 1 in front)
    • Is it a PREFIX (kilo, centi, milli, micro, nano)?
      • If YES ‚Üí Use the DIRECT (Prefix to Base) table:
        • Find "1 [your prefix]" in the left column (Example: "1 centi")
        • Follow that row to the right past the = sign
        • Copy the exponent number (the small number after 10) into your denominator next to the 10
        • Draw a line through both matching units to cancel them
      • If NO (it's a base unit like m, g, or L) ‚Üí Use the INVERSE (Base to Prefix) table:
        • Find "1 (m, g, L)" in the left column
        • Scan the right column for your TARGET prefix (Example: "centi")
        • Copy the exponent from that row into your denominator next to the 10
        • Draw a line through both matching units to cancel them
  9. Step 2: Move the exponent in the denominator to the numerator.
    • If the sign was negative (-) change it to positive (+).
    • If the sign was positive (+) change it to negative (-).
  10. Step 2 (cont): Don't forget the unit of measure.
  11. Step 3: Solve the math by adding exponents.
  12. Step 4: Write your final answer and draw a box around it.
